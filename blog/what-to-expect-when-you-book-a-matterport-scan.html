What to Expect When You Book a Matterport Scan

If you've been thinking about getting a Matterport 3D tour for a listing, a commercial space, or a property you manage — but you're not quite sure what the process involves on your end — this is the quick rundown. No surprises, no complexity. Here's what happens from the moment you reach out to the moment your tour is live.

Step 1: Book Your Scan

You can schedule online, call me at 864.351.4255, or send an email to james@southeast3dtours.com. I'll need three things: the property address, the approximate square footage, and your preferred date and time. That's it.

If you're not sure about the square footage, a rough estimate is fine — it just helps me plan the scan time and give you an accurate quote. You can check exact pricing anytime on the pricing page.

Most scans are scheduled within a few days. Same-week availability is common.

Step 2: Prepare the Space

The Matterport camera captures everything it sees, so the space should look the way you want it to appear in the final tour. For most properties, that means:

  • All lights on
  • Blinds and curtains open
  • Ceiling fans off
  • Toilet lids down
  • Personal items, clutter, and trash out of sight
  • Pets secured or off-site during the scan

If you're scanning a residential listing, treat it the way you'd prep for a professional photo shoot. For commercial spaces, make sure the space is clean and any areas you don't want captured are closed off.

For the full checklist, take a look at the Scan Prep Guide.

Step 3: The Scan Itself

I show up with the Matterport camera and a tripod. No crew, no lighting rigs, no production setup. The camera is placed at multiple positions throughout the space — each scan point takes about 20 seconds — and moves room to room until the entire property is captured.

How long does the scan take? It depends on the size of the space:

  • A 1,500 sq ft home typically takes about 45 minutes to an hour
  • A 3,000 sq ft property runs closer to 90 minutes
  • Larger commercial spaces — 5,000+ sq ft — may take 2–3 hours

You don't need to be present for the scan. Many agents hand off the lockbox code or meet me at the door and head to their next appointment. The process is quiet and unobtrusive — no one needs to vacate the building, and there's no disruption to neighboring units or businesses.

Step 4: Processing and Delivery

Once the scan is complete, I upload the data and your tour is processed. Standard turnaround is 24–48 hours. When it's ready, you'll receive:

  • A shareable link to your interactive 3D tour — works on any device, no app required
  • An embed code if you want to put the tour on your website or a listing page
  • Access to the dollhouse view and floor plan view
  • The built-in measurement tool, active for anyone who opens the tour

If you added a schematic floor plan ($75) or aerial photography ($150), those are delivered at the same time.

Your tour is hosted on Matterport Cloud for 12 months at no additional charge. After that, you can renew hosting or transfer the tour to your own Matterport account — also free. Full pricing details are on the pricing page.

Step 5: Use It Everywhere

The tour link is yours to share however you want. Drop it in an MLS listing. Text it to a prospective buyer. Add it to a vacation rental listing on VRBO. Email it to an insurance adjuster. Post it on social media. Send it to an out-of-state client who can't visit in person.

It works 24/7, on any device, with no login or download required. One link, unlimited views.

That's the Whole Process

Book → Prep → Scan → Receive your tour in 24–48 hours → Share it everywhere.

No complicated setup. No ongoing commitment. No technical expertise needed on your end. If you've ever hired a photographer for a listing, the process is simpler than that.
