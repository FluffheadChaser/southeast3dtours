Commercial Property 3D Tours: How Matterport Virtual Tours Help Lease Retail, Office, Industrial, and Restaurant Spaces Faster

Commercial real estate moves differently than residential. The deal cycles are longer, the decision-makers are busier, and the prospects are often hundreds of miles away. A Matterport 3D tour doesn't just look impressive — it solves real problems that cost landlords and brokers time and money.

Whether you're leasing a strip mall end cap, a Class B office suite, a 20,000-square-foot warehouse, or a turnkey restaurant space, here's how a 3D virtual tour changes the game.

The Problem With Commercial Showings

In commercial real estate, a showing isn't just an inconvenience — it's a significant investment of time for everyone involved. The prospect has to travel (sometimes from another city), coordinate schedules with a decision-making team, and carve out hours in the middle of a workday. The broker has to prep, drive, and often make multiple trips for the same space.

And after all of that? The prospect may walk in, take one look at the layout, and realize the space doesn't work for them. Wrong ceiling height. Wrong storefront exposure. Columns in the wrong place. It's a common story, and it's entirely preventable.

A Matterport 3D tour lets prospects do that first "gut check" visit entirely online — before anyone books a flight or clears a calendar.

Retail Spaces: Show the Flow Before the Footprint

For retail tenants, the physical layout of a space is everything. They need to visualize where merchandise goes, how customers will flow through, where the checkout counter lands, and whether the storefront has the presence they need. Square footage numbers on a flyer don't tell that story. A 3D walkthrough does.

A virtual tour lets retail prospects:

  • Walk the floor plan from the entrance to the back of house
  • Assess storefront visibility and window placement
  • Evaluate ceiling heights, column locations, and electrical panel access
  • Share the tour with business partners, franchisors, or investors who aren't local

The result: tenants arrive at in-person showings already sold on the layout. They're there to negotiate, not to evaluate.

Office Space: Win the Out-of-Market Tenant

The commercial office market has been through a significant reset since 2020, and the tenants who are actively looking for space today are often doing it remotely first. Corporate expansion decisions are made by executives in Atlanta or Charlotte before anyone local ever sets foot in a building.

A 3D virtual tour of your office space gives those remote decision-makers:

  • A realistic sense of the floor plan and suite configurations
  • Accurate spatial measurements using Matterport's built-in measuring tool
  • The ability to share the tour with HR teams evaluating employee commute considerations
  • Confidence in their shortlist before flying in for final walkthroughs

In a market where office brokers are competing hard for fewer active tenants, a virtual tour signals that you're a serious operator who makes the leasing process easy.

Industrial and Warehouse Space: Where the Details Live

Industrial tenants are buying specifications, not aesthetics. They need to know dock door counts, clear height, column spacing, power availability, and truck court depth. But before they can evaluate any of that in person, they need to make a trip — sometimes a long one.

Matterport's 3D tours are particularly valuable for industrial spaces because:

  • The schematic floor plan export gives tenants accurate footprint dimensions
  • 3D scans capture dock doors, overhead clearances, and drive-in access visually
  • Multi-building or multi-unit campuses can be documented comprehensively
  • Logistics companies and manufacturers evaluating multiple markets can compare properties remotely before narrowing down

For landlords with vacancy in multiple markets, a 3D tour library means your available inventory is always showing — 24 hours a day, to any prospect, anywhere.

Restaurant Spaces: The Most Layout-Sensitive Lease in Commercial Real Estate

Restaurant tenants have perhaps the highest sensitivity to physical space of any commercial user. Kitchen layout, hood placement, grease trap access, dining room flow, bar position, restroom compliance, and ADA pathways all need to work in concert. A space that looks right on paper can be completely wrong in person.

For restaurant spaces — especially second-generation (already built-out) units — a Matterport 3D tour can be the difference between attracting the right tenant quickly and watching the space sit. Restaurateurs evaluating multiple markets or second-gen sites will prioritize tours that let them bring in their architect or kitchen designer remotely to assess feasibility before flying in.

If your space has existing hood infrastructure, a walk-in cooler, or a Type I hood — capture it in 3D. That detail closes deals.

It Works for Your Listing Marketing, Too

Beyond the showing process itself, a 3D virtual tour strengthens every part of your marketing stack:

  • CoStar and LoopNet listings stand out with an interactive tour link
  • Email campaigns to tenant rep brokers include a clickable walkthrough
  • Your website or property page gives prospects a reason to stay and engage
  • Social media posts with tour previews generate more engagement than static photos

Commercial real estate is a relationship business, but it's also an information business. The listing that gives prospects the most useful information fastest will win more showings — and more leases.
