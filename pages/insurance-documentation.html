Most Property Owners Discover Their Documentation Gap After the Damage Is Done

Here is the scenario that plays out thousands of times a year across the Southeast: a storm tears through, a pipe bursts, a fire breaks out. The property owner calls their insurance company and begins the claims process. The adjuster asks for documentation of the property's pre-loss condition. And all the owner has is a handful of phone photos from the day they moved in, some receipts in a drawer, and their memory of what the place looked like.

That gap between what was actually there and what you can prove was there is where claims get reduced, disputed, or denied. The countertops that were upgraded last year. The custom built-in shelving. The hardwood floors under the carpet. Without comprehensive documentation, you are negotiating with one hand tied behind your back.

A Matterport 3D scan creates a complete digital twin of your property. Every room, every fixture, every finish, every personal item visible in the space. It is navigable, measurable, and time-stamped. When the adjuster asks what was there before the loss, you do not have to remember. You show them.

6 Reasons Pre-Loss Documentation Is the Smartest Investment You Can Make

1. Document What Photos Cannot Capture

A photo shows one wall from one angle. A 3D scan captures every surface, every corner, and every room in navigable, measurable detail. Adjusters can walk through the property as if they were standing inside it. They can see the crown molding, the light fixtures, the flooring transitions, and every finish that contributes to your property's value.

2. Prove the Value of Upgrades and Improvements

That $40,000 kitchen renovation. The custom closet system. The finished basement. If you do not have documentation showing these improvements existed before the loss, you are claiming against the original builder-grade finishes. A 3D scan captures every upgrade in immersive detail, ensuring your claim reflects the true value of your property.

3. Comprehensive Contents Documentation

Walk through your pre-loss scan and every visible item is right there. Electronics, furniture, artwork, collectibles, tools, equipment. Instead of trying to remember every item from a burned or flooded home, you can navigate through the scan room by room and build a complete inventory from documented evidence.

4. Eliminate Disputes Over Property Condition

Was the roof in good condition before the storm? Were the walls already showing water stains? Was the deck structurally sound? Without pre-loss documentation, these questions become your word against the adjuster's assessment. With a 3D scan, the answers are permanent, visual, and irrefutable.

5. Share With Multiple Parties Simultaneously

Your insurance company, your public adjuster, your contractor, and your attorney all need to see the property's pre-loss condition. Instead of coordinating site visits that can no longer happen, share the 3D scan link. Everyone can walk through the property independently, take measurements, and reference specific details on their own schedule.

6. Peace of Mind That Costs Less Than a Single Night's Stay

For the price of one night at a hotel, you get a permanent, comprehensive record of your entire property. If you never need it, you spent $350 on peace of mind. If you do need it, it could be the difference between a full settlement and a fraction of what you deserve. That is the best insurance you will ever buy.

Questions Property Owners Ask About Insurance Documentation

When should I get a pre-loss documentation scan?

The best time is now, before you need it. Ideally, schedule a scan after moving in, after major renovations, or after purchasing high-value items. Seasonal property owners should scan before storm season. The scan only has value if it exists before the loss occurs.

Will my insurance company accept a Matterport scan as documentation?

Matterport 3D scans are increasingly used in the insurance industry for both pre-loss documentation and post-loss claims processing. The scans are time-stamped, measurement-accurate, and provide comprehensive visual evidence that adjusters and attorneys recognize as reliable documentation.

Can the scan capture contents and personal property?

Yes. The 3D scan captures everything visible in the space. Furniture, electronics, artwork, appliances, tools, collectibles, and any item that is out and visible. For insurance purposes, we recommend having your valuable items displayed or placed where they can be seen during the scan.

How detailed is the scan? Can you see finishes and materials?

The scan captures photorealistic detail. You can identify countertop materials, flooring types, cabinet finishes, light fixtures, hardware, and other details that matter for replacement cost estimates. Adjusters can zoom in on specific areas and take measurements directly inside the scan.

Should I also get a scan after a loss occurs?

Absolutely. A post-loss scan documents the damage in the same immersive 3D format. When paired with a pre-loss scan, the comparison makes the scope of damage immediately clear. This combination is extremely powerful for claims processing and dispute resolution.

I am an insurance agent. Can I offer this to my clients?

Yes. Many insurance agents recommend pre-loss documentation as a value-added service for their policyholders. We work with agents and agencies across the Southeast and can set up referral programs or portfolio pricing for your client base. Call us at 864.351.4255 to discuss.

How long is the scan accessible online?

Standard hosting is 12 months with renewal options, or transfer to your own Matterport account for free. For insurance documentation, we recommend maintaining access as long as you own the property. We can also help you archive scans for offline access if needed. The documentation is only valuable if you can access it when you need it.

What areas do you serve?

We serve parts of South Carolina, North Carolina and Georgia. Same-day service is available in the greater Greenville area. For locations across the Southeast, we schedule regular trips throughout the week. Contact us to schedule your documentation scan.
