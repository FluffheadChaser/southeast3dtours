6 Reasons Smart Builders Document Every Project in 3D

1. Pre-Drywall Documentation That Saves You in Court

Once the drywall goes up, the framing, plumbing, electrical, and HVAC behind it become invisible. A pre-drywall 3D scan captures every stud, every pipe, every wire run in navigable 3D. If there is ever a question about what is behind that wall, you have the answer. Permanently.

2. Progress Tracking That Clients Actually Understand

Daily logs and spreadsheets do not mean much to a homeowner building their dream house. But walking through their home in 3D, seeing the framing go up, watching the finishes come together over multiple scans? That builds trust, reduces anxiety, and keeps the relationship strong through a long build cycle.

3. Remote Monitoring for Multi-Site Teams

If you are running three job sites and cannot be at all of them every day, 3D scans let you virtually walk each site from your office. Check progress, spot issues, and coordinate subs without burning hours on the road. Your project managers can review scans before morning meetings and arrive on site already informed.

4. Warranty Documentation That Protects You Long-Term

When a homeowner calls two years after closing claiming a defect was there from day one, your final walkthrough scan shows exactly what was delivered. As-built documentation is your best warranty defense. It eliminates he-said-she-said and replaces it with documented evidence.

5. Win More Bids With Professional Documentation

When you show prospective clients that you document every phase of construction in 3D, you communicate professionalism, transparency, and accountability. That separates you from every other builder who still relies on phone photos and verbal updates. Clients choose builders they trust.

6. Accurate As-Built Records for Future Renovation

Your 3D scans become a permanent digital record of the property. When the owner wants to renovate five years later, the architect does not have to guess what is behind the walls. They can reference your as-built scans with accurate measurements. That is a service that keeps your name on their lips.

Questions Builders and Contractors Ask Us

When is the best time to schedule a construction documentation scan?

The most critical scan is pre-drywall, after rough-in of mechanical, electrical, and plumbing. Other high-value milestones include foundation completion, framing, insulation, and final walkthrough. Many builders schedule 3-5 scans across a typical project to create a complete visual timeline.

Can you scan an active job site with crews working?

Yes, though we get the best results when the area is clear of active work. Most builders schedule scans at the end of a phase, before the next sub crew arrives. We can also scan early mornings or during lunch breaks. The scan itself does not interfere with work.

Are the scans admissible as documentation in disputes?

Matterport scans are time-stamped, measurement-accurate, and create an immersive record of conditions at the time of capture. They have been used successfully in construction disputes, insurance claims, and legal proceedings. Many attorneys and adjusters recognize them as reliable visual evidence.

How accurate are the measurements in the 3D scan?

Matterport measurements are accurate to within 1% under standard conditions. That is precise enough for documentation, space planning, and verifying dimensions. For exact survey-grade measurements, we recommend using the scans alongside traditional surveying methods.

Can I share scans with my architect, engineer, or client?

Absolutely. Each scan generates a shareable link that works in any web browser. No downloads, no special software. You can share it with your entire project team, subcontractors, inspectors, or clients. Everyone can walk through the scan and take measurements independently.

Do you offer volume pricing for recurring scans?

Yes. Many builders book a series of scans across a project lifecycle. We offer package rates for multi-scan projects and portfolio pricing for builders running multiple jobs simultaneously. Call us at 864.351.4255 to discuss a custom documentation plan.

How long are the scans stored and accessible?

Standard hosting is 12 months, with renewal options available, or transfer to your own Matterport account for free. For construction documentation, many clients opt for extended hosting to maintain records through the warranty period. We can also help you download and archive scans for your permanent project files.

What areas do you cover for construction documentation?

We serve parts of South Carolina, North Carolina and Georgia. Same-day service is available in the greater Greenville area. For projects in Charleston, Columbia, Asheville, and surrounding areas, we schedule regular trips and can accommodate recurring milestone scans.
