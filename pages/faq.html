About Matterport & the Technology

New to 3D virtual tours? Start here.

A Matterport 3D tour is a photorealistic, navigable digital replica of a real-world space. Viewers can walk through it in a web browser on any device — clicking through rooms, zooming in on details, and exploring at their own pace. It's the closest thing to physically being inside a property without actually going there. Read our full guide to what Matterport is and how it works →

Photos are static — you see what the photographer chose to show you. Video is linear — you follow a path someone else decided. A Matterport tour is interactive: viewers choose where to go, how long to stay, and what to look at. They can also switch between a 3D walkthrough, a Dollhouse View from above, and a floor plan layout. It's a fundamentally different level of engagement.

The Dollhouse View is Matterport's signature feature — a three-dimensional aerial view of the entire property that you can rotate and zoom. It gives viewers an immediate sense of scale, layout, and how rooms connect. Nothing else in property marketing offers this perspective.

Yes. Matterport's built-in Measurement Mode lets viewers measure distances between any two points in the tour. It's accurate to within about 1% — useful for checking furniture fit, ceiling height, or doorway widths before visiting in person.

Any modern smartphone, tablet, or computer with a web browser. No app download required. Tours also work with VR headsets for a fully immersive experience.

Standard hosting is included for 12 months. After that you can renew hosting with us or transfer the tour to your own Matterport account at no additional charge. See hosting options →

Commercial, Insurance & Specialty Uses

Beyond residential real estate — professional and institutional applications.

Yes. We scan office buildings, retail spaces, restaurants, hotels, multifamily properties, warehouses, and any other commercial space. Remote tours are especially effective for commercial leasing — investors and tenants in other cities can tour a space without flying in. Commercial property 3D tours →

A pre-loss Matterport scan creates a complete, time-stamped visual record of your property before any damage occurs. If fire, flood, wind, or theft strikes, you have navigable proof of exactly what existed — contents, finishes, fixtures, layout. This evidence strengthens claims and reduces disputes. Insurance documentation details →

Yes. 3D scans have been used in personal injury cases, property disputes, accident reconstruction, and insurance investigations. Because the scan preserves scene conditions at a specific moment in time with precise LiDAR measurements, it serves as a credible evidentiary record that photographs alone can't provide. Legal & forensic documentation →

We scan the same property at key milestones throughout the build — framing, MEP rough-in, drywall, and finish stages. Each scan is a time-stamped record of exactly what was built at that point. Useful for project management, owner communication, dispute resolution, and documentation of work behind walls. Construction documentation →

Reality capture is the process of using LiDAR and 3D scanning to create precise digital records of physical spaces — typically for architects, engineers, contractors, facility managers, and large property owners who need accurate spatial data rather than just a marketing tour. The output can include point clouds, floor plans in DXF/DWG format, and BIM-compatible files. 3D Scanning & Reality Capture →
